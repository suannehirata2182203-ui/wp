Choosing the Right Shipping Service

Published: February 1, 2025 Updated: March 15, 2025

Choosing the right courier can make all the difference for fragile shipments

Why the Courier Matters

I learned the hard way that not all shipping services treat fragile packages the same. My first three shipments went through the Polish postal service (Poczta Polska), and while two arrived safely, one was completely destroyed. The difference wasn't in my packaging—it was in how the packages were handled.

Since then, I've tested major shipping services available in Poland, sending similar packages through different couriers to compare results. Here's what I discovered.

Services I've Tested

Over the past three years, I've used six different shipping services for fragile packages. Here's my honest assessment of each:

DHL Express

DHL has been my most reliable option for international fragile shipments. They offer a "Fragile" handling option that actually seems to work—packages marked with this option receive gentler handling.

Pros:

  • Dedicated fragile handling service
  • Excellent tracking and updates
  • Fast delivery times (usually 2-3 business days within Europe)
  • Good customer service when issues arise

Cons:

  • More expensive than other options
  • Requires proper customs documentation

I've sent 12 packages through DHL, and all arrived intact. The tracking system is excellent, and I've found their customer service responsive when I had questions.

FedEx International

FedEx offers similar services to DHL, with comparable pricing. I've used them for five shipments, and all arrived safely. Their handling seems professional, though I haven't noticed as much difference between their standard and fragile handling options.

Pros:

  • Reliable delivery
  • Good tracking system
  • Multiple service levels available

Cons:

  • Similar pricing to DHL (expensive)
  • Less obvious fragile handling differentiation

UPS

I've used UPS for three shipments. While all arrived safely, I found their tracking less detailed than DHL or FedEx. Their pricing is competitive, but I prefer the transparency of DHL's service.

Pros:

  • Competitive pricing
  • Reliable delivery
  • Wide coverage

Cons:

  • Less detailed tracking
  • Customer service can be slow to respond

Poczta Polska (Polish Post)

As Poland's national postal service, Poczta Polska is the most affordable option. However, my experience has been mixed. Out of eight shipments, two arrived damaged. The service is slower (often 7-14 days for international delivery) and tracking is less reliable.

Pros:

  • Very affordable
  • Widely available throughout Poland
  • No need for account setup

Cons:

  • Inconsistent handling of fragile items
  • Slower delivery times
  • Less reliable tracking
  • Limited insurance options

I still use Poczta Polska for less valuable items or when budget is a primary concern, but I always purchase insurance and use extra packaging.

InPost

InPost is popular in Poland for domestic and some international shipments. I've used them for four packages, and while all arrived safely, their international options are more limited than major couriers.

Pros:

  • Affordable pricing
  • Convenient parcel lockers
  • Good for domestic shipments

Cons:

  • Limited international destinations
  • Less specialized fragile handling

DPD

DPD is another European courier I've tested. I've sent three packages through DPD, and all arrived safely. Their pricing is mid-range, and service is reliable, though I prefer DHL for high-value fragile items.

Pros:

  • Good European coverage
  • Competitive pricing
  • Reliable service

Cons:

  • Less specialized fragile services
  • Tracking less detailed than premium services

What to Look For

Based on my experience, here are the key factors to consider when choosing a shipping service for fragile packages:

Fragile Handling Options

Not all services offer dedicated fragile handling, and those that do may not always follow through. DHL's fragile option seems to be the most reliable, but always mark packages clearly regardless of the service you choose.

Insurance Coverage

Always purchase insurance for fragile packages. Most services offer insurance up to a certain value, and it's worth the extra cost. I've had to file two insurance claims, and both were processed without major issues.

According to shipping industry standards referenced by ShipMatrix, proper insurance coverage is essential for high-value fragile shipments.

Tracking Quality

Good tracking gives you peace of mind and helps identify problems early. DHL and FedEx offer the most detailed tracking, showing exactly where your package is at each stage.

Customer Service

When something goes wrong, you need responsive customer service. I've found DHL and FedEx to be the most helpful when issues arise, while Poczta Polska's customer service can be slower to respond.

My Recommendations

For high-value fragile items: Use DHL Express with fragile handling option. The extra cost is worth the peace of mind.

For mid-value items: FedEx or UPS offer good balance between cost and reliability.

For budget-conscious shipments: Poczta Polska can work, but use extra packaging and always purchase insurance.

For domestic Polish shipments: InPost is convenient and affordable, with good parcel locker network.

Red Flags to Avoid

Some services I've encountered that I wouldn't recommend for fragile items:

  • Services that don't offer insurance
  • Couriers with poor tracking systems
  • Services that don't allow fragile markings
  • Extremely cheap services that seem too good to be true
